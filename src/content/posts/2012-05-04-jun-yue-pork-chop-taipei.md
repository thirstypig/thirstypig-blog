**What is Jun Yue?**
Jun Yue is a small local chain of restaurants, specializing in pork chops, based in Taipei. The restaurants are small and have a fast food quality about them, with the chairs and table attached to the floor. I’ve been to three of the branches, all on the east side of Taipei. When I used to live in Taipei, it was a simple goto restaurant to go to with friends. Not a place to impress a date.

**What did I eat?**
Jun Yue specializes in pork chops – Taiwanese style deep fried pork chops. We each had a modestly sized pork chop, lightly breaded, lightly salted, and chopped up each bone in. With each meal, you can pick either soup noodles or rice with a small bowl of soup. I, of course, choose rice. The fixins are also a treat – which you can add to your meal like – tofu with thousand year old egg, egg tofu, hardboiled tea eggs, and a small array of cold vegetable dishes.

**What I didn’t particularly like.**
Coming from the US, I think I would have preferred a larger portion of the deep fried pork chop. Additionally, I would have also like braised pork over rice, another Taiwanese favorite, as another option. But that’s more my stomach talking. Regardless the food was very good.

**The Last Word**
Jun Yue is what it is. It doesn’t try to be more or less. Is it a place where I talk out-of-towners? Probably not. But is it place I know where I can be happy for lunch or a light dinner, with old friends? Sure.
